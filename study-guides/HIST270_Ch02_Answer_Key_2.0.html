Part III — Key Terms: Model Sketch + Quote Support

Each answer includes a short quote (≤25 words) + a locator.
Spring and Autumn Period
Answer: 770–479 BCE; many states; hegemon order attempts.
“Spring and Autumn Period 770–479 BCE”
Source: Lecture Deck 1, slide “The Multistate System Emerges”
Warring States Period
Answer: 479–256 BCE; fewer big states; free-for-all warfare.
“Warring States Period 479–256 BCE”
Source: Lecture Deck 1, slide “The Multistate System Emerges”
shi (士)
Answer: educated advisor class; itinerant problem-solvers for states.
“Different philosophies = different ‘job applications.’”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “The Hundred Schools of Thought”
ren (仁)
Answer: inner virtue; genuine care in relationships.
“Ren without Li is ineffective.”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “The Solution: Ren 仁 and Li 禮”
li (禮)
Answer: proper conduct/ritual across life; shapes character through practice.
“Li without Ren is empty.”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “The Solution: Ren 仁 and Li 禮”
junzi (君子)
Answer: cultivated “gentleman” ideal; model for virtue-based governance.
“Governance Through Virtue”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “Governance Through Virtue”
Jian ai / Universal Love
Answer: impartial care; rejects strong hierarchy of obligation.
“Jian ai 兼愛 - Universal love”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “Universal Love vs. Hierarchical Love”
Mencius
Answer: beginnings of virtue; human nature starts good; nurture matters.
“Humans are born with beginnings of virtue:”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “The Four Sprouts 四端”
Xunzi
Answer: human nature bad; ritual discipline corrects; bridge toward Legalism.
“Core argument: Human nature is fundamentally BAD”
Source: Lecture Deck 2, slide “Xunzi 荀子 - The Realist”
Dao (道)
Answer: the Way; ultimate pattern beyond ordinary language.
“The Dao that can be spoken is not the eternal Dao.”
Source: Lecture Deck 3, slide “The Dao 道 - The Way”
wu-wei (無為)
Answer: effortless action; harmony without forcing.
“Literally ‘non-action’ but really effortless action”
Source: Lecture Deck 3, slide “Wu-wei 無為 - Non-Action”
Legalism
Answer: state power through law/rewards/punishments; a winning strategy for Qin.
“Legalism is the philosophy of the state that WINS.”
Source: Lecture Deck 3, slide “Legalism: Philosophy of Winners”
