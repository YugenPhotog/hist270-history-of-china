Part I — The 4 Big Takeaways (Short Answers — No Paragraphs)

Keep these four in your head while you do Parts II–VI. If you can’t connect an answer to a takeaway, you’re probably memorizing trivia.

1) Collapse → competition → a crisis of order Lecture Emphasis

  • When Zhou authority collapses, violence spreads.
  • States compete without a shared “referee.”
  • Thinkers sell plans to restore order.

2) Warfare changes everything: armies, taxes, administration Lecture Emphasis

  • War expands beyond elite chariots.
  • States mobilize populations and resources.
  • Government grows more centralized and systematic.

3) The “Hundred Schools” are competing answers, not trivia Lecture Emphasis

  • Confucians: virtue and ritual reform society.
  • Mohists: impartial care, practical utility, merit.
  • Daoists/Legalists: radical alternatives to “try harder.”

4) Legacy: Qin wins with Legalism; debates outlive Qin Lecture Emphasis

  • Legalist tools help Qin unify.
  • Confucian ideals shape later legitimacy.
  • These frameworks remain political templates.
