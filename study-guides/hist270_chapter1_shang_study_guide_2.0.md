\## Part II — Common Mistakes to Avoid



\### Directions

Put a checkmark next to each one after you understand it.



\- \[ ] Shang legitimacy is \*\*NOT\*\* the Mandate of Heaven.  

\- \[ ] Mandate of Heaven is \*\*NOT\*\* “divine right forever.” It can be lost.  

\- \[ ] Zhou “feudal-like” rule is \*\*NOT identical\*\* to medieval European feudalism.  

\- \[ ] Sanxingdui is \*\*NOT\*\* “later than Shang.” It overlaps with Shang time.  

\- \[ ] Oracle bones are \*\*NOT\*\* myths — they are written primary evidence.  

\- \[ ] “China” was \*\*not naturally unified\*\* from the beginning; unity is constructed.

\### Big Takeaway #1 — Evidence

\- Inscribed bones/shells used for divination  

\- Records questions about war, harvest, childbirth, sacrifice  

\- Proves Shang history using direct written evidence  



\### Big Takeaway #2 — Power

\- King monopolized ritual connection to spirit world  

\- Sacrifice and bronzes reinforced elite hierarchy  

\- Oracle bone divination guided major state decisions  



\### Big Takeaway #3 — Legitimacy

\- Zhou needed justification after overthrowing Shang  

\- Solution: Mandate of Heaven as legitimation theory  

\- Bad rulers lose mandate; overthrow becomes explainable  



\### Big Takeaway #4 — Diversity

\- Sanxingdui = coeval, sophisticated Bronze Age culture  

\- Challenges Yellow River-only origin story  

\- Distinctive masks/bronzes show regional difference
