Part II — Common Mistakes to Avoid

Check each one after you can explain why it’s wrong.
Sui “failed” because it built nothing useful. (It built lasting infrastructure and institutions.)
Sui collapse was “just bad leadership.” (Overreach + crises + rebellion stacked up.)
Tang was purely “Chinese” and closed to outsiders. (It recruited and governed a multi-ethnic empire.)
Exams instantly replaced aristocrats. (They created a new path, but didn’t erase pedigree.)
Empress Wu was just an “empress consort.” (She declared herself emperor of a new dynasty.)
An Lushan was an isolated “bad guy.” (Rebellion grew from Tang frontier structures and policies.)
After 755, everything simply “declined.” (Politics transformed; culture and trade continued.)
Regional governors were minor officials. (Jiedushi held real provincial power.)
